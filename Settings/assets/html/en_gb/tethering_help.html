USB tethering

You can tether your phone to your computer with a USB cable, to share your phone's internet connection with your computer
  • Tethering works with Windows Vista, Windows 7 and Linux

  • You will be unable to mount your phone's SD card on your computer when tethered via USB

  • For more information, including using USB tethering with other OSs, see http://www.android.com/tether#usb

Mobile Wi-Fi hotspot

You can turn your phone into a mobile Wi-Fi hotspot, to share your phone's internet connection with one or more computers or other devices
  • When your phone is acting as a Wi-Fi hotspot, you will be unable to use your phone's applications to access the internet via its Wi-Fi connection

  • You can configure the hotspot in Wi-Fi hotspot settings, which include: setting the client start address, the channel that can be used and setting the Wi-Fi hotspot as broadcast or hidden.

  • You can view the connected clients, disconnect clients and limit the number of clients that can be connected. You can also specify which clients can connect to the Wi-Fi hotspot using manage devices.

  • You can adjust the delay to turn off the Wi-Fi hotspot automatically if there is no client data activity.

  • For more information, visit http://www.android.com/tether#wifi
