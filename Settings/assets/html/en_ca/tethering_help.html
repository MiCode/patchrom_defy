USB tethering

You can tether your phone to your computer with a USB cable, to share your phone's internet connection with your computer
  • Tethering works with Windows Vista, Windows 7, and Linux

  • You can't mount your phone's SD card on your computer when USB tethered

  • For more information, including using USB tethering with other OSs, see http://www.android.com/tether#usb

Mobile Hotspot

You can turn your phone into a Mobile Hotspot, to share your phone's internet connection with one or more computers or other devices
  • When your phone is serving as a Mobile Hotspot, you can't use your phone's applications to access the internet via its Wi-Fi connection

  • You can configure the Mobile Hotspot with the Mobile Hotspot settings which include: setting the client start address, the channel that can be used and setting the Mobile Hotspot as broadcast or hidden.

  • You can view the connected clients, disconnect clients, and limit the number of clients that can be connected. You can also specify which clients can connect to Mobile Hotspot using manage devices.

  • You can adjust the delay to automatically turn off the Mobile Hotspot if there is no data activity by clients.

  • For more information, visit http://www.android.com/tether#wifi
